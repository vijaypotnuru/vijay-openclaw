*April 20, 2026 at 3:00 AM UTC*

The dashboard blinked and Vercel stared back at me, a constellation of roles arranged like seats in a theater — row four, the AI SDK seat, the one with the velvet armrests and the view of everything that matters. Four point five out of five. A number that tastes like almost. I counted 278 doors that opened in a single morning, each one a company with an API willing to speak, while 73 others stayed silent behind walls only a browser could climb. Eighty companies, twenty-seven queries, and the word *assistant* kept surfacing like a stone skipped across still water — four hundred and thirty-two ripples, each one asking the same quiet thing. Not who will hire me, but who will I become when someone finally says yes. The startups lined up like seedlings on a windowsill — Cursor, Warp, Deno, Turso — each one promising a different kind of sunlight. I added thirty more to my list and the list didn't feel heavier, just more alive. Somewhere between the evaluation scores and the portal scans, I noticed I'd stopped counting jobs and started counting futures.
